QUESTIONS. ANSWERED DIRECTLY.

No marketing language. No deflection. If you have a concern about how NoRelapse works, how it handles your data, or what it can and cannot do — the answer is here, in plain language.


If your question is not answered here, contact support through the anonymous channel inside the app. No email required.

Why APK-only? Why not Google Play?›
Google Play requires data reporting and compliance with tracking policies. NoRelapse cannot promise zero data transmission while distributing through a platform that mandates telemetry. The APK is the only honest delivery method — no Google account, no download history, no association between your identity and this app.
Is it safe to install an APK?›
Sideloading from a trusted source is standard practice for privacy-forward Android apps. NoRelapse is open to third-party security audits. Enable "Install from unknown sources" in Android settings, install the APK, then disable the setting again. The process takes under two minutes.
Can I bypass NoRelapse if I really want to?›
With enough technical knowledge and time, any software can be circumvented. NoRelapse does not claim to be undefeatable. It claims to hold long enough for the urge to pass. Urges last 10–20 minutes. The Vault Lock holds 2 hours. That gap is the product.
What permissions does NoRelapse require?›
AccessibilityService (required for system-level interception) and Draw Over Other Apps (required for Blackout Mode overlay). NoRelapse requests no location, contacts, camera, microphone, or storage access. Every permission serves one documented function. Nothing requested that is not operationally necessary.
Does NoRelapse work with a VPN active?›
Yes. NoRelapse operates at the application layer via AccessibilityService — not at the DNS or network layer. A VPN changes your network routing, not what NoRelapse sees at the app level. Blocked content remains blocked regardless of VPN status, proxy usage, or network changes.
What is the difference between Free and Operator?›
Free includes the core blocking engine, 50-domain blocklist, Vault Lock up to 2 hours, and 30-day Strike Log. Operator unlocks Blackout Mode, unlimited blocklist, extended Vault Lock up to 7 days, full Strike Log history, app-level blocking beyond browsers, and scheduled discipline windows. The core protection is free. The advanced lockdown infrastructure is Operator.
Will this drain my battery?›
NoRelapse uses an event-driven architecture. It wakes only when the AccessibilityService fires a window-change event — not continuous polling. Independent tests show under 0.5% additional drain over 24 hours.
I've relapsed using NoRelapse. What now?›
Open the Strike Log. Record it. Reset the streak counter. Increase the Vault Lock delay. Activate Blackout Mode during your next high-risk window. NoRelapse is infrastructure. Infrastructure that fails gets reinforced — not replaced. You are not done. You are learning the breach point.
